Selected Projects

Spectroscopic Temperature Measurements to Characterize Solid-State Ion Sources
Justus Liebig University
[PDF]

In my bachelor thesis, I developed and automated a temperature measurement setup using optical emission spectroscopy to characterize solid-state ion sources. The Richardson-Dushman equation relates temperature to emitted current density, and I applied this to the study of crystal emission properties. My results confirmed the expected behavior of the produced crystals and demonstrated the method's potential for optimizing their production.

Ion Optics Simulation of a GIE Extraction Grid
Justus Liebig University
[PDF]

Gridded ion engines (GIE) use an extraction grid to accelerate ions to the desired velocity. I performed simulations of ion optics using IBSimu, a Linux-based framework, to determine optimal grid distances. By comparing different figures of merit, I identified the optimal configuration for maximizing thrust efficiency.

Impact of Error Mitigation on the Quantum Teleportation Circuit
University of Washington
[PDF]

This project focused on enhancing the accuracy of quantum teleportation circuits through the application of error mitigation techniques. I worked with a team to implement and evaluate two different error correction schemes, analyzing their effect on the fidelity of quantum information transfer.

Augmented Apollo Powered Descent Guidance Paper Discussion
University of Washington
[PDF]

In this project, I analyzed and reproduced a novel powered descent guidance system for lunar and martian landings. I recreated key elements of the guidance system and visualized its performance, providing an in-depth analysis of its potential advantages and limitations. This work deepened my understanding of spacecraft control systems and their application in precision landings for deep space exploration.
